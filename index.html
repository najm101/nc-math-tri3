Formulas & rules

4-3 · Coordinates in 3-D

  • Distance: √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)² + (z₂−z₁)²]
  • Midpoint: ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2, (z₁+z₂)/2)

4-4 · Special right triangles

  • 45-45-90: the two legs are equal; hypotenuse = leg × √2
  • 30-60-90: short : long : hyp = 1 : √3 : 2 (short is opposite 30°, long opposite 60°, hyp opposite 90°)

4-5 · Trigonometric ratios

  • SOH-CAH-TOA: sin = opp/hyp · cos = adj/hyp · tan = opp/adj
  • Finding an angle from two sides: pick the ratio that uses them, then take the inverse — θ = sin⁻¹(…), cos⁻¹(…), or tan⁻¹(…).

4-6 · Applications of trigonometry

  • Angles of elevation and depression are measured from the horizontal; the angle of depression (looking down) equals the angle of elevation (looking up) because the lines are parallel.
  • Area of a triangle: ½ · a · b · sin C — two sides and the angle between them.

4-7 · Law of Sines

  • sinA/a = sinB/b = sinC/c
  • Find a side, or an angle: sinC = c·sinA/a, then C = sin⁻¹(…).
  • Ambiguous case (SSA): height h = c·sin A. If a < h → 0 triangles; a = h → 1; h < a < c → 2; a ≥ c → 1.

4-8 · Law of Cosines

  • c² = a² + b² − 2ab·cos C
  • Use it with two sides and the included angle (SAS), or all three sides (SSS).

5-1 · Circles & circumference

  • Circumference: C = 2πr or C = πd
  • diameter = 2 × radius · radius = diameter ÷ 2

5-2 · Angles & arcs in circles

  • A whole circle = 360°; a semicircle (across a diameter) = 180°.
  • An arc's measure equals its central angle. Major arc = 360° − minor arc.
  • Arc length = (central angle ÷ 360) × circumference
  • From a circle graph: arc° = (percent ÷ 100) × 360
  • Degrees → radians: × π/180 · Radians → degrees: × 180/π

5-3 · Arcs & chords

  • Chords equidistant from the center are congruent — and congruent chords are equidistant. Set the two equal measures equal and solve for x.
